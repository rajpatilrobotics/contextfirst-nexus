# Vetted Source Register

## 1. Purpose

This register records the public sources allowed to shape ContextFirst Nexus product requirements, guidance cards, safety rules, technical architecture, testing approach, and public framing.

It prevents three common errors:

- treating international guidance as automatic domestic law;
- presenting operational indicators as an AI classification rule;
- implying endorsement or validation by an issuing organization.

The register is not a case-law database and does not provide legal advice.

## 2. Source status labels

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Challenge rule | Controlling public event requirement or official track description |
| Primary international instrument | Treaty text or other primary international legal instrument |
| Authoritative guidance or report | Official guidance, analysis, or recommendations from a competent institution |
| Operational indicator | Practitioner-oriented indicator set that supports further inquiry |
| Voluntary risk framework | Non-certifying AI risk-management guidance |
| Security guidance | Application-security guidance used for threat controls |
| Technical documentation | Official provider, framework, library, or testing documentation used for implementation decisions |
| Provider terms | Official service, privacy, retention, training-use, or data-control terms that constrain provider use |
| Accessibility standard | Published accessibility standard used as a design and testing target |
| Context only | Background material that cannot support a product or legal conclusion by itself |

## 9. Register rules

### 9.1 Source selection

- Prefer the issuing body's official page or stable PDF.
- Use secondary reporting only for discovery, not as the final authority when a primary source exists.
- Record publication or version date and last-verified date.
- Recheck challenge rules and live web guidance before final submission.
- Archive only short attributed excerpts and precise page or section locators unless redistribution permission is clear.

### 9.2 Product guidance cards

Every in-product guidance card must include:

- source ID;
- issuer;
- title;
- date or version;
- jurisdiction or scope;
- source type and binding-or-guidance label;
- exact excerpt or faithful short paraphrase;
- direct URL;
- last verified date;
- legal verification required notice when applicable.

### 9.3 Legal hierarchy

- International instruments and guidance may inform practitioner questions.
- They do not automatically determine domestic law.
- Operational indicators may organize inquiry.
- They do not create model labels or individual findings.
- Context reports may explain why a workflow matters.
- They do not prove a fact in a case packet.
- Responsible-AI and security frameworks shape controls.
- They do not certify the product.

### 9.4 Separation from case evidence

The interface and exports must keep case sources separate from guidance sources.

A guidance passage cannot corroborate a fact about Maya K. A case document cannot establish the meaning or current validity of domestic law.

### 9.5 Prohibited source claims

The project must not say:

- UN-approved;
- OHCHR-compliant;
- OSCE-validated;
- ILO-certified;
- NIST-certified;
- OWASP-secure;
- judge-endorsed;
- partner co-designed;
- legally verified across jurisdictions.

The project may say, when accurate:

- designed with reference to the published sources listed in this register;
- uses a versioned international-guidance pack for qualified review;
- requires domestic legal verification;
- reports measured synthetic-fixture results.

## 10. Sources intentionally excluded from product truth

The following must not enter product requirements or public claims unless separately vetted and approved:

- social-media posts or inferred preferences of judges;
- private Google Drive or parent-folder research copied wholesale;
- unpublished NGO material;
- personally identifying information about organizers or reviewers;
- anonymous blogs, marketing comparisons, or unsourced statistics;
- real survivor narratives used as demo content;
- provider marketing used as proof of safety or legal compliance.
